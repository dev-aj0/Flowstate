Beta/alpha power ratio over time: With vs without NeuroCoach

Ratio ≥ 1.2 = focused (alertness > relaxation). With NeuroCoach the ratio stays above threshold; without the app it drops below.

How we measured it

  • Device: Muse 2 headband, 4 EEG channels (TP9, AF7, AF8, TP10) at 256 Hz.
  • Signal: Raw EEG buffered in 1-second windows, Hann-windowed, FFT → band power (delta 0.5–4 Hz, theta 4–8 Hz, alpha 8–13 Hz, beta 13–30 Hz, gamma 30–100 Hz). Each band = % of total power.
  • Metric: β/α ratio = beta power ÷ alpha power. Beta ≈ alertness/sustained attention; alpha ≈ relaxed wakefulness/mind-wandering. Ratio ≥ 1.2 → “focused”; < 1.2 → “not focused.”
  • Chart: Mean β/α ratio per 5-minute block over a 25-minute session. Dashed line = 1.2 threshold.
  • Calibration (optional): Participants could run focus/relax calibration to set a personal threshold.
