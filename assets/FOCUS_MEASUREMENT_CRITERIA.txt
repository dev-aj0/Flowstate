Focus was measured using EEG from a Muse 2 headband (4 channels: TP9, AF7, AF8, TP10, sampled at 256 Hz). Raw signals were buffered in 1-second windows, Hann-windowed, and transformed with an FFT into standard frequency bands (delta 0.5–4 Hz, theta 4–8 Hz, alpha 8–13 Hz, beta 13–30 Hz, gamma 30–100 Hz). Each band’s power was expressed as a percentage of total power. We used the beta/alpha power ratio (β/α) as the focus metric: beta is linked to alertness and sustained attention, alpha to relaxed wakefulness and mind-wandering. A ratio at or above 1.2 (or a participant-specific threshold after calibration) was classified as focused; below 1.2 as not focused. The comparison chart plots mean β/α ratio per 5-minute block over a 25-minute session, with a reference line at 1.2. Participants could optionally complete a short focus/relax calibration to set a personal threshold.
