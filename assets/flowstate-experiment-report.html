Results and analysis

What we found

In this small pilot, both participants averaged slightly higher focus and fewer distraction alerts during FlowState (study days 4–6) than during the control days (1–3) without the device. The shifts were modest—a few percentage points on the focus index and roughly two fewer alerts per 30-minute session on average. With only two people, we describe a possible pattern, not proof that FlowState caused the change.

Figures

Figure 1 shows mean focus and mean alerts for each person in each phase, with bars for session-to-session spread. Figure 2 shows scores by calendar day; numbers moved around from day to day, but after the switch to FlowState there was a small upward step rather than a smooth line. The β/α ratio generally moved with the focus reading, which fits how the app is built. Figure 3 plots each session’s focus against alerts; FlowState sessions tended toward higher focus and fewer alerts than most control sessions. For a poster or write-up, these three figures are enough to tell the story—you do not need every number in a table.

How the prototype worked

In the FlowState phase, participants wore a Muse headband. The app used beta and alpha power from the EEG to show a focus percentage and to count alerts when focus fell below a set level. A wristband gave short haptic feedback. In control, they did the same homework without the headband or app, so the comparison is “device on” versus “device off,” not a harder or easier assignment.

Statistics

We used descriptive statistics only (means and standard deviations), not tests like t-tests or ANOVA. Two participants and three sessions per phase is too small for those tests to be meaningful. The error bars in Figure 1 show how much the three sessions varied within each phase; they are not confidence intervals for a large population.

Limits

These results apply only to this homework block and schedule. The values in this report are set up as an example for charts and wording; swap in your own real session data when you submit, and follow your competition rules about sharing raw data.

Operational definitions

  • Focus % — Session mean of the in-app focus meter (β/α-derived vs calibration threshold).
  • Alerts — Count of threshold-based refocus events per 30 min.
  • β̄, ᾱ — Mean band power as % of total FFT power (Muse → FlowState backend).
  • β/α — Ratio of mean beta to mean alpha power; co-varies with focus index but is not identical to the UI %.
